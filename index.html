Assistant Professor
Mathematical and Physical Sciences Division
School of Arts and Sciences
Ahmedabad University, India

My research lies at the intersection of number theory, quantum computation, and mathematical physics. My work focuses on modular forms, automorphic representations, and the design of scalable quantum algorithms. I am particularly interested in developing mathematically grounded frameworks for quantum computing and exploring their applications in signal processing, optimization, and scientific computing.

Research

Quantum Algorithms

Design of scalable and resource-efficient quantum algorithms including phase estimation, amplitude estimation, and Grover-type search algorithms.

Number Theory

Research on modular forms, Eisenstein series, automorphic representations, and connections to arithmetic geometry.

Quantum Information Processing

Development of hybrid classical-quantum algorithms for signal processing, image processing, and numerical computation.

Computational Mathematics

Mathematical frameworks for numerical integration, optimization, and differential equations using quantum computing.

Selected Research

Towards Practical Quantum Phase Estimation

A modular and scalable quantum phase estimation framework designed to reduce circuit depth and improve adaptability for near-term quantum hardware implementations.

  • Read Paper

Efficient Quantum Algorithm for Weighted Partial Sums

This work introduces a quantum algorithm for computing weighted partial sums and performing numerical integration with improved efficiency compared with classical approaches.

  • Read Paper

Efficient Implementation of Quantum Search

A practical implementation of Grover-type search algorithms for arbitrary database sizes, removing the power-of-two constraint present in standard formulations.

  • Read Paper

Hybrid Classical-Quantum Image Processing

Introduces a hybrid computational framework combining classical image processing with quantum circuits based on Walsh basis functions.
